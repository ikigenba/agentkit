# Phase 24 — Migrate to message-granular delivery: remove the consumer delta surface

*Realizes design Decision 2 (the message-granular `Event` taxonomy — the deletion of `R-C7MI-HRFI` and the minting of `R-HUZX-7N2W`, plus the reworded surviving ids), Decision 9 (the SPI change: `RoundTrip` drops `Events()` and exposes only the assembled `Message()`; `R-DRFX-VNF2` reworded), and Decision 10 (the orchestration loop no longer forwards deltas). Depends on Phase 05 (the orchestration core, `Stream`/`Send`/SPI it modifies), Phase 11 (the Google adapter whose text-vs-reasoning classification `R-DRFX-VNF2` re-asserts), and the adapter phases 08–11 it edits.*

Carry out the product decision to stop delivering replies token-by-token (audit Finding 4, Option B): the consumer no longer receives sub-message fragments, only completed units. This is a cross-cutting refactor in one accumulating context spanning the root package and all four adapters (the shape of Phase 22's atomic retype), because the delta types are referenced across the SPI seam.

End state:

- The consumer `Event` taxonomy (root `orchestration.go`) carries only whole events — `ToolUse`, `ToolResult`, `MessageDone`. The `TextDelta` and `ReasoningDelta` types are **removed**. A turn's visible answer and reasoning summary are read from `MessageDone.Message` (its `TextBlock`s and `ReasoningBlock.Summary`), never as streamed fragments.
- The provider SPI `RoundTrip` (root) **drops the `Events()` method** and its backing `events iter.Seq[Event]` field; `NewRoundTrip` drops the events parameter. Each adapter (`anthropic`, `openai`, `google`, shared `internal/openaicompat` backing `zai`) stops building the precomputed delta slice and constructs its `RoundTrip` from the assembled `Message` + metadata alone. Backend SSE decoding is unchanged — each adapter still consumes the provider's incremental `text/event-stream` to assemble that `Message`, and still closes the round-trip body before returning it (Decision 12 / `internal/sse` retained verbatim).
- The orchestration loop (`runTurn`) no longer iterates `rt.Events()` to forward deltas; it takes `rt.Message()`, emits `MessageDone`, and proceeds to the tool loop exactly as before. The atomic-rollback, re-entrancy, and early-break cleanup paths are unchanged (early `break` now lands at a message boundary).
- No reference to `TextDelta`, `ReasoningDelta`, or `RoundTrip.Events` survives anywhere in non-test or test source; `go build ./...` and `go vet ./...` are clean.

**Done when:** R-HUZX-7N2W is covered by a clearly-named test (a text-only turn yields exactly one `MessageDone` whose `Message` carries the full assembled answer text and **no** `*Delta` events, after which `Err()==nil` and `Usage()` is populated); the surviving D2 ids R-C8UE-VJ67, R-CBA7-N2NL, R-CCI4-0UEA, R-CDQ0-EM4Z remain covered with the delta types gone (early-break cleanup `R-CCI4-0UEA` now exercised at a message boundary); the reworded D9 id R-DRFX-VNF2 is covered by a test asserting a Google visible-text part bearing a `thoughtSignature` lands as a visible `TextBlock` in the assembled `Message` (not reasoning); the orchestration coverage that previously cited `R-C7MI-HRFI` (Phase 05) is updated to the message-granular assertions; and the full suite is green per design Conventions.
